    • Big IDEA

    We would like to encourage everyone to use their special skills in addressing Food Security through efforts for Environment Sustainability using the 8 multiple intelligences.
    Image from WWOOF Philippines

    Environment Sustainability and Food Security are two of the most pressing issues that we have to address today. Since then, the common notion is to rely to the government, institutions and to our farmers in solving the crisis. While they are the frontliners and their efforts are substantial, we all have to realize that we ourselves are also responsible, and are capable in creating the much needed push to aid the plight. We can be heroes too! In light of this, like the ASEAN motto says, “One Vision, One Identity, One Community”, we have do our part and move together as One.

    Moreover, overtime, we have relied on specific skills and knowledge thinking that these are the only way. Again, while these create a strong foundation in providing solutions, we have to remember that there are more than 8 multiple intelligences (Naturalist, Musical, Logical-mathematical, Existential (life smart), Interpersonal, Bodily-kinesthetic, Linguistic, Intra-personal, Spatial). If we will be able to tap these individual uniqueness and appreciate our diversity, we will be able to create more innovative solutions and a variety of approaches.

    With this, we would like to encourage the ASEAN Youth (15 - 35 years old) to use their special skills in addressing Food Security through efforts for Environment Sustainability using the 8 multiple intelligences. This will be called
    8 Acts of Agricooltability wherein they will be given a platform to hone whatever blessing they have by practicing it in alignment to our goal. They can share it to the world via online platforms like Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram. From all the posts with hashtags #FutureReadyASEAN #Agricooltability, community managers will select the cycle’s winner. They'll get featured, and receive green currencies.

    Big WHY

    In 2014, Dr Robert S. Zeigler said that across Southeast Asia the typical age of farmers is 50s and 60s . On the other hand, the median age of the current population is in the 20s. Still true, this shows the huge gap between our food producers, and the growing number of people to feed.

    The growing number of people to feed.
    Image by Gerd Altmann from Pixabay

    Moreover, few are attracted to agriculture because of the extensive hard work and the uneducated perception tagged to farmers in third world countries. However, ironically in farmers in first world countries are usually educated, experienced and enjoys its profitability.

    Furthermore, there’s more than 600 Million People in Southeast Asia and it’s expected to reach 721 million by 2030. Evidently added to the number of farmers and their aging sector, there’s a gigantic pressure to provide supply.

    Sustainable Development Goal Number 2: Zero Hunger
    Image from ISGlobal - Barcelona Institute for Global Health

    It calls for a collective action towards Food Security. This is
    Goal Number 2: Zero Hunger in the 17 Sustainable Development Goals set by the United Nations General Assembly in 2015 for the year 2030.

    In the 21st century, we have proven that technology is a double edge sword. It is up to us on how we will utilize this powerful tool. With the right information and intention, we could solve societal problems that years ago was not available.
